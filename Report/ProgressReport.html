Progress Report

Our approach to The 2110 Portal

The 2110 Portal began with the idea to highlight workplaces within United Auto Workers Union (UAW) Local 2110, as it is an influential union within the New York City area that represents workers from over 40 workplaces and spans multiple industries and types of cultural institutions. (View UAW 2110’s list of workplaces here.) UAW Local 2110 has been involved in many recent unionization efforts within the city, some influenced by the pandemic, and all by the various needs of today’s workforce. Our website hopes to answer the question of why cultural institutions seek unionization and how demands of different industries within Local 2110 compare.

As we began the process of creating our first interactive digital piece of journalism, we based much of our thinking off of the key takeaways in the MIT Open Documentary Lab report, “Mapping the Intersection of Two Cultures: Interactive Documentary and Digital Journalism.” The takeaways that influenced our project most were the following:


Let story determine form (Urrichio, 6)

We wanted our website to be as engaging on a human and emotional level as possible. Unionization is at its heart the standing together of a group of people. Although there is much data associated with this story, especially when analyzing union agreements, we wanted the user to come away with an experience of the people who are making advancement of workplace rights possible. To do so, we decided to embed audio clips of our interviewees throughout the narrative on our website, in hopes of actively reminding the reader who they are reading about as they consume the information.


Experiment and learn (Urrichio, 6)

The most time-consuming aspect of this project was learning how to use interactive tools to accomplish our goals. We mainly used Twine, Soundcite, and StoryMap, but we also had a learning curve implementing certain aspects of HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. However, now that we have gone through the process of making this project, it will be much easier to incorporate these skills into future journalistic endeavors.


Shape conversations and Consider long-term impact (Urrichio, 6)

UAW Local 2110 covers many industries, yet their site does not sort workplaces by industry or need. As seen in unionization trends throughout the past few years, when employees at one workplace accomplish a goal like getting higher pay or receiving health insurance, there is often a trickle effect once other workplaces learn of what is accomplished. We wanted to provide a source that breaks down needs and gives insight into the unionization process by industry. The hope is that those who are looking to unionize in their workplace, or even those who are not, will be able to read about the needs and accomplishments of their industry and enact change in their own workplace in an informed manner. We also hope this site makes unionization seem less intimidating and more possible for users to implement in their own lives, grounded in the human experience as it is.


Our process creating The 2110 Portal

Collecting Raw Material and Soundcite Audio Clips

We began the process of collecting audio by researching the workplaces and industries represented by UAW Local 2110 and contacting union representatives or workers who had recently unionized. We decided to focus on recently unionized workplaces to highlight the current trends and needs of workers. Audio interviews were conducted with workers from the New Museum of Contemporary Art, Barnard Contingent Faculty, and HarperCollins Publishers to represent the arts industry, academia, and the media industry respectively. Topics of interest included motivation to unionize, process and outcomes of unionization, remaining needs, and changes to be made in the larger industry.

These interviews were edited into clips and published to Internet Archive, and the program Soundcite was used to create code for inline audio players for each clip. The clips were incorporated into articles on each industry’s page on Twine for the user to listen to while reading about the industry.

Videos and photos of each workplace were collected and incorporated into the Twine pages of each industry to help place the reader in the location.
